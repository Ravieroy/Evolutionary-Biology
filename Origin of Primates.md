### Some hypothesis for evolution of primates
Source: [Major Hypotheses About Primate Origins | LibreTexts](https://socialsci.libretexts.org/Bookshelves/Anthropology/Biological_Anthropology/EXPLORATIONS%3A__An_Open_Invitation_to_Biological__Anthropology_1e/08%3A_Primate_Evolution/8.02%3A_Major_Hypotheses_About_Primate_Origins)
1. Angiosperm Coevolution Hypothesis: (Bob Sussman) suggests that ==flowering plants and primates evolved together in a mutually beneficial relationship==. This relationship is called coevolution.
2. Visual-Predation Hypothesis: (Matt Carmill) suggests that primates developed grasping hands and good vision to catch insects. ==Critics of the hypothesis argue that insectivory is uncommon among extant primates, so it is unlikely to have been a major influence on early primate evolution.==

Why did H. Neanderthals and Denisovans became extinct?
1. Needed more diet for the large brains(1200-1740 cc) which meant competing with carnivores. 
2. Neanderthals lived in small families until 40 thousand years. This resulted in significant inbreeding. Therefore small numbers and changing climate brought these species to extinction.
