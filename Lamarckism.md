*Jean-Baptiste Pierre Antoine de Monet, Chevalier de Lamarck*

![[22-lamarckism.png | 400x300]]

Lamarck hypothesized that different organisms originated separately by spontaneous generation from non-living matter, starting at the bottom of the chain of being. A ”nervous fluid” acts within each species that causes the organism to progress up the chain. The species originate at different times and therefore we see a hierarchy of species.

He argued that species differ from one another because they have different needs and so use some of their organs and appendages more than others. More used organs get enlarged by the “nervous fluid”.  He believed that the changes acquired during one’s lifetime gets inherited, a principle called *inheritance of acquired characteristics*.

In the famous example of giraffes neck, Lamarck's theory suggested that giraffes must have stretched their necks to reach the higher food sources and over the course of generations their necks were lengthened. The longer necks were inherited, and over the course of generations, this process was repeated and their necks got longer and longer. 

While his idea of evolution was wrong he was the first to advance a coherent and testable [[Theory]] of evolution.
