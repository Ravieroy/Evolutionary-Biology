## 1. Comparative Anatomy
Comparative anatomy analysis in [[Evolution]] is a method used to study the similarities and differences in the anatomical structures of different species. The key concepts are:
1. *Homologous Structures :* Structures that are similar in different species because they were inherited from a common ancestor, though they may have different functions.
2. *Analogous Structures :* Structures that have similar functions in different species but are not derived from a common ancestor, arising from convergent evolution.
3. *Vestigial Structures :* Structures that have lost their original function but remain in some species, providing evidence of evolution from a more ancient ancestor.[^1][^2]

It is one of the lines of evidence in [[Biological anthropology]] and very useful in understanding the [[Origin of Primates]].
