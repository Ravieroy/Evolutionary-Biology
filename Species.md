## Definition
### Def. 1
> *A species is often defined as a group of individuals that actually or potentially interbreed in nature. In this sense, a species is the biggest gene pool possible under natural conditions.*
> 
> [Berkeley | EVO 101](https://evolution.berkeley.edu/evolution-101/speciation/defining-a-species/)

### Def. 2
> *A [species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Species) is a group of organisms that can freely interbreed with each other, but cannot produce fertile offspring with other, related organisms.* 

![[04-species-happyface-spiders.png]]

Happy face spiders _look_ different, but since they can interbreed, they are considered the same species: _Theridion grallator_.

## Is the definition of species really cut and dried?
1. The definition of a species as a group of interbreeding individuals cannot be easily applied to organisms that reproduce only or mainly asexually.
2. Hooded crows and carrion crows look different, and largely mate within their own groups — but in some areas, they hybridize. Should they be considered the same species or separate species?
