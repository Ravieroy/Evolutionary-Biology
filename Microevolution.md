## Definition
### Def. 1
> *Microevolution is evolution on a small scale — within a single population. That means narrowing our focus to one branch of the tree of life.*

![[02-microevolution.png | 400]]

[Evolution Berkeley | Evo 101](https://evolution.berkeley.edu/evolution-101/microevolution/defining-microevolution/)

#### Sub-Def. 1 
> population is a group of organisms that interbreed with each other — that is, they all share a gene pool. 

#### Explanation
If two individuals of a species mate, their genetic information combines into a new set of chromosomes during the process which we know as Meiosis. During this process, there are always random genetic mutations which are caused by things like gene-overlapping, crossing, exchanging.

This means that the offspring can have new genetic traits that were not apparent in any of the parents. Those changes can be phenotypical or aphenotypical.

## Detecting microevolutionary change
![[02b-detecting-microevolution.png|400]]

A change in gene frequency over time means that the population has evolved.

## Causes of microevolution
1. Mutation (could be random)
	- *since any particular mutation is rare, this process alone cannot account for a big change in allele frequency over one generation.*
	Types of mutations
		1. point mutation
		2. frameshift mutation
		3. deletion/insertion
		4. translocation
		5. gene depletion
		6. chromosome duplication
		7. entire genome duplication
		8. chromosome inversion etc. 

2. Gene flow/ Migration

![[02c-gene-flow.png | 400]]

3. Natural selection 
	- *Beetles with brown genes escaped predation and survived to reproduce more frequently than beetles with green genes, so that more brown genes got into the next generation.*
![[02d-natural-selection.png | 400]]

4. Random genetic drift 
	- *just by random luck more brown genes than green genes ended up in the offspring. For example a tree branch fell and killed only a particular type.*
![[02e-genetic-drift.png|400]]
