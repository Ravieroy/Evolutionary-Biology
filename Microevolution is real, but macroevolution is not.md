## Random Walk Counterargument
It describes the behavior of objects subject to random forces, like dust particles in the air. In a random walk, objects and systems will drift over time. We don’t know _where_ they will drift, but we know they will. It turns out that the amount of drift is the roughly square root of the number of steps (some useful measure of time). When applied to evolution if a thousand generations of intelligent, directed, selective breeding can produce all of the species of dogs, then, having established that it’s possible under _any_ circumstance, in a random walk, we should expect that a _million_ generations of random variation can do the same thing—millions of years.
