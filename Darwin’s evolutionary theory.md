> *"If variations useful to any organic being ever occur, assuredly individuals thus characterized will have the best chance of being preserved in the struggle for life; and from the strong principle of inheritance, these will tend to produce offspring similarly characterized. This principle of preservation, or the survival of the fittest, I have called natural selection"*
> - Charles Darwin

1. *Evolution* is the simple proposition that the characteristics of organisms change over time.
2. The idea of *common descent* is different from [[Lamarckism]] and Darwin was first to argue that species had diverged from common ancestors and that the species can be portrayed as one great family tree representing actual ancestry.
3. *Gradualism* is a proposition that the differences between even radically different organisms have evolved by small steps through intermediate forms, not by leaps.
4. *Populational change* is Darwin’s hypothesis that evolution occurs by changes in the proportions of different variants of individuals within a population.
5. *Natural selection* accounts for adaptation, features that appear “designed” to fit organisms to their environment. It provided a natural mechanism as an explanation for the adaptive design that was previously attributed to a divine intelligence.

Darwin could never provide solution to the question of “where do the hereditary variations come from?” However he was not aware of the work of Gregor Mendel who had solved this problem.
