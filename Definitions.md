| :--------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| [[Allele]]                   | An allele is a variant form of a gene.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Allele frequency             | The proportion of gene copies in a population<br>that are a given allele.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Animal                       | Multicellular, heterotrophic eukaryotes that produce collagen.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Adaptation                   | a feature that has become prevalent in a<br>population because of a selective advantage conveyed by that<br>feature in the improvement in some function.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| [[Baramin]]                  |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Benefit                      | Refers to the impact of a phenotype on the likelihood that organism with that phenotype will pass their genes onto future generations versus organisms with other phenotypes.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                         |
| [[Clade]]                    | The set of species descended from a particular ancestral<br>species.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| [[Evolution]]                | In its simplest form evolution is a change in [[Allele]] frequencies within a population over time. More simply it is =="descent with modification"==. Even simpler it is =="Biological change over generations"==                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Gene                         | A region of DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) coding either for the messenger RNA encoding the amino acid sequence in a polypeptide chain or for a functional RNA molecule.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| [[Gene Flow]]                | Exchange of alleles between two populations.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Genetic Drift                | Change in allele frequency due to random chance.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Genotype                     | Refers to the genetic makeup of an organism; in other words, it describes an organism's complete set of genes.([Nature](https://www.nature.com/scitable/definition/genotype-234/))                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| [[Grade]]                    | A group of species that have evolved the same<br>state in one or more characters and typically constitute<br>a paraphyletic group relative to other species that have<br>evolved further in the same direction.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| Isolated System              | A system that engages in **no** exchanges of energy or matter with the surroundings.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| [[Macroevolution]]           | Macroevolution is evolution on a grand scale — what we see when we look at the over-arching history of life. Biological change over generations outside the species.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| [[Microevolution]]           | Microevolution is evolution on a small scale — within a single population(species). That means narrowing our focus to one branch of the tree of life.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| [[Mutation]]                 | It is a change in a genetic sequence. It includes changes as small as the substitution of a single DNA building block, or nucleotide base, with another nucleotide base.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| [[Natural Selection]]        | The differential survival and/or reproduction of classes of entities that differ in one or more characteristics. To constitute natural selection, the difference in survival and/or reproduction cannot be due to chance, and it must have the potential consequence of altering the proportions of the different entities.                                                                                                                                                           |
| Negative Selection           | Form of natural selection in which rare, deleterious alleles are removed from a population.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| [[Phenotype]]                | Refers to the observable physical properties of an organism; these include the organism's appearance, development, and behavior. An organism's phenotype is determined by its genotype, which is the set of genes the organism carries, as well as by environmental influences upon these genes.([Nature](https://www.nature.com/scitable/definition/phenotype-phenotypes-35/#:~:text=The%20term%20%22phenotype%22%20refers%20to,environmental%20influences%20upon%20these%20genes.)) |
| Point mutation               | A change in a single nucleotide in a DNA sequence                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Primordial Soup              | This is a term for conditions that may have existed on Earth before the life first appeared.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Retroviruses                 | RNA viruses that reverse-transcribe their genomes into a host's DNA. It reproduces by inserting itself directly into the cell's DNA.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Ring Species                 | Ring species are a continuous loop of related populations, each adapted to its local environment, with two terminal populations in the loop meeting but now unable to mate.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
| Second Law of Thermodynamics | In an *isolated system*, a process can occur only if it increases the total entropy of the system.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Speciation                   | An evolutionary process by which a new species comes into being.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| [[Species]]                  | A common definition is, "A group of individuals that are able to produce viable, fertile offspring".                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| [[Theory]]                   | A scheme or system of ideas or statements held as an explanation or<br>account of a group of facts or phenomena; a hypothesis that has been confirmed or established by observation or experiment, and is propounded or accepted as accounting for the known facts; a statement of what are held to be the general laws, principles, or causes of something known or observed.(*Richard Dawkins, The Greatest Show on Earth, Ch. 1*)                                                  |
| Transitional features        | A fossil that shows an intermediate state between an ancestral trait and that of its later descendants is said to bear a transitional feature.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
