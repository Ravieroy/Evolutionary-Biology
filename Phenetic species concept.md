#level_2 
A species is a set of organisms that are phenotypically similar and that look different from other sets of organisms. According to this concept, phenotypic similarity is all that matters in recognizing separate species. Since the frogs depicted here look the same — even though they are prevented from mating with each other — they would be considered the same species according to the phenetic species concept.
